IOT of Things & Smart Cities:

In the connected world we live in today, the notion of Internet of Things (IoT) is ubiquitous. Students engaged with this topic will be introduced to the notion of cloud, connectivity of institutions, role of sensors in generating data and information, integration of all the data from multiple devices and sources and finally using this data to design smart and efficient cities and countries.

Laser & FIber Optic Communications:

Student builds a laser communications setup, using a laser pointer and a simple modulator for the laser power, under guidance. With the setup, any audio signal, such as a favorite music or a speech, may be sent over the laser beam to a distant receiver module, also built by the student. The signal from the receiver is then given to a standard speaker, through which the audio signal can be heard. Communications with a laser beam work only in the direction of "line-of-sight", and in situations where the signal has to bend around corners, or in smoky or dusty conditions, the signal has to be carried via an optical fiber. The student experiments with an optical fiber to learn skills of beam alignment and coupling into the fiber, and output coupling to a receiver, and then to a speaker.

Data Analytics & Artificial Intelligence:

Students working in the area of data analytics will explore multiple aspects related to data including how data is generated, how much data is generated, how data is processed, analyzed and presented. The subject matter experts design the content that is suitable to a young audience using a medium that they can relate to. Examples include data collection from social media, wearable devices and using software tools and artificial intelligence techniques to process and present the findings.

What is Discovery Spot?:

An iSpace, Inc. initiative, Discovery Spot is a technology playground and project center in which students of any age can experience cutting edge technology, participate in any of our hands on activities, or develop their own projects with the help of our friendly and knowledgeable instructors, tools, and resources. We offer group camps and group activities to allow students to immerse themselves in all things STEM, as well as individual activities and use of lab equipment for students to continue their passions. Content is developed by subject matter experts at the University of West Florida, whereas iSpace provides software development expertise and financial support. Discovery Spot differentiates itself from the typical STEM Camp through its variety and breadth of technologies, its incorporation of industry ready skills, as well as coordinating development and participant interaction with professors at the University of West Florida and software expertise provided by iSpace Inc. Each activity is developed in accordance with current academic research, but is also planned and executed to be the most interesting and impactful for the students who learn from it. Additionally, Discovery Spot is a continually evolving concept, i.e we ensure that we introduce students to the most current topics in science and technology that advances society. .
